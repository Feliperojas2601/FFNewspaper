Humans of UNAL When I was in my second semester, I had to take a course called “Mass Balance”. The professor, whose name was Hugo Galindo, was known because he had a very quick temper. And also he liked to speak to the students in a sarcastic way, and that made us feel scared or small.
One day, he asked us to write a 500 word essay, based on a lecture he had sent to us about climate change. The lecture was not so difficult, and it could be understood without being a bright person. However, he tended to be very exigent when he marked the essays, then it was such an achievement to get more than 3.5 in the final score. I was really nervous when I sent the essay, because I was thinking that he may not like it and I would get a bad mark. But, for a certain reason, I felt kind of confident in what I’ve written, because I was resourceful and mixed climate change with environmental philosophy, and the words just came to me while I was writing.
A week after sending the essay, the professor showed up to the classroom very sympathetic, and began to talk about how our texts had mostly liked him (that surprised us all). Then he mentioned that someone had sent the best essay that he had ever read. Suddenly, he pointed to me with his finger, and he said that I had been that person who wrote the best essay. I could barely believe it, and everyone started clapping me.
Certainly, it was an experience that made me feel successful, and it's a memory that I will always remember.
